AI Fundamentals

Skills and Innovation Academy (SIA)

Defining AI

  • AI enables machines to think, learn, and act like humans
  • It analyzes data, recognizes patterns, and makes decisions

AI is the science of building systems that mimic human intelligence and automate decision-making.

Machine Learning (ML)

  • ML allows computers to improve over time
  • Examples: spam filters, recommendation engines, fraud detection

ML is a subset of AI that learns from data—without being explicitly programmed.

Deep Learning

  • Uses layered neural networks inspired by the human brain
  • Powers speech recognition, image generation, and NLP

Deep learning enables breakthroughs in understanding language, images, and sound.

Natural Language Processing (NLP)

  • NLP helps machines interpret and generate text
  • Applications: chatbots, translation, summarization

NLP bridges the gap between human communication and machine understanding.

Computer Vision

  • Enables interpretation of visual data
  • Used in facial recognition, self-driving cars, medical imaging

Computer vision allows machines to extract meaning from images and video.

Generative AI

  • Generates text, images, music, and more
  • Examples: ChatGPT, DALL·E, Midjourney

Generative AI learns patterns and creates original outputs based on training data.

Ethical and Responsible AI

  • Promotes fairness, transparency, and safety
  • Reduces bias and supports responsible innovation

Ethics in AI ensures that technology benefits society and respects human values.

The Future of AI

  • AI is reshaping education, healthcare, business, and creativity
  • Foundational knowledge prepares learners for tomorrow

AI is everywhere—learning its fundamentals empowers you to lead and innovate.
