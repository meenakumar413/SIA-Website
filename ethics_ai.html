Ethics in Generative AI

Generative AI & Ethics: The Urgency of Now

This module explores how generative artificial intelligence (Gen AI) raises new ethical challenges and how organisations can respond responsibly. Based on the LinkedIn Learning course by Vilas Dhar, it empowers learners to analyse, plan and lead with ethics at the centre of generative AI deployment.

1. Why Ethics Matters in Gen AI

As tools gain creative ability, they also raise questions of fairness, ownership, bias, accountability and human values.

2. Ethical Analysis for AI

Learn frameworks for distinguishing where responsibility lies: with design, data, leadership or stakeholders.

3. Organising Your Organisation for Ethical AI

From data governance to C-Suite strategy and board oversight — see how ethics needs to live across the organisation.

4. Stakeholders & Engagement

Including your customers, users and broader society in how Gen AI is designed and deployed builds trust and resilience.

5. Continuous Ethical Mindset

Ethics isn’t a one-off task — it’s a continuous practice of review, adaptation and questioning.

6. Key Ethical Themes

Transparency, accountability, fairness, privacy, security, human-centric values and inclusion are core to responsible Gen AI.

7. Organisational Alignment

Ethical AI aligns business strategy, technology choices and social purpose — ensuring Gen AI adds value responsibly.

8. Putting Ethics into Practice

Use tools, governance, stakeholder feedback and education to embed ethics into your AI lifecycle.
