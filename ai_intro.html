Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

Welcome!

This presentation covers key concepts on AI fundamentals, designed for professionals and students entering the AI space.

What Is Artificial Intelligence?

  • General vs. narrow intelligence
  • Strong AI vs. weak AI
  • The general problem-solver concept

AI is the simulation of human intelligence in machines. This slide introduces foundational definitions and distinctions.

Predictive vs. Generative AI

  • Predictive AI: forecasts outcomes
  • Generative AI: creates new content
  • Use cases in business and creativity

Predictive AI helps with decisions, while generative AI powers tools like ChatGPT and image generators.

Machine Learning Basics

  • Supervised vs. unsupervised learning
  • Labeled and unlabeled data
  • Massive datasets and model training

Machine learning is the engine behind AI. It learns patterns from data to make predictions or decisions.

Neural Networks

  • Layers of nodes and weights
  • Activation bias and learning from mistakes
  • Backpropagation and accuracy improvement

Neural networks simulate how the brain processes information. They’re key to deep learning systems.

Common AI Systems

  • Robotics and automation
  • Natural language processing
  • Internet of Things (IoT)

AI is embedded in many systems—from smart assistants to industrial robots and connected devices.

Popular Algorithms

  • K-nearest neighbor
  • K-means clustering
  • Regression and Naive Bayes

These algorithms help classify, cluster, and predict data. Each has strengths depending on the problem.

Generative AI Architecture

  • GANs and VAEs
  • Transformers and LLMs
  • Image diffusion models

Generative models create realistic outputs—text, images, and more—using advanced architectures.

Ethical & Legal Challenges

  • Alignment problem
  • Decision traceability
  • Copyright and privacy concerns

With great power comes responsibility. Ethical AI design is critical to avoid bias and misuse.
